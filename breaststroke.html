Breastroke

The Chest style, also known as the Breaststroke, is considered the slowest of the swim styles recognized by the International Swimming Federation. The chest is the first style that swimmers learn since a calm and pleasant technique is used. But it is also one of the styles that costs the most to master since it requires a lot of coordination in the movements. About 50% of the work is distributed in the upper body and the lower body, since the kick plays a very important function when swimming.


Techniques:

Grip:

This phase begins with the arms extended forward and with the hands together. From there, the hands are directed up and out, laterally, directing the palms in the same direction of movement.

Pull:

The elbow is then flexed and internal rotation of the arms occurs. Hands go back and in.

Return:

Finally the elbows are extended to bring the hands forward. The palms are facing and parallel in the forward direction so that they do not offer resistance, until they are placed in the initial grip position
