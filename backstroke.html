Backstroke

In swimming, the back style is very similar to the crawl, only this time the swimmer floats with his back facing the water. The sequence of movements is worked alternately, that is, one arm in the air with the palm of the hand coming out from under the leg, while the other propels the body in the water.


Techniques:

The movement of the arms:

The movement of the arms from a global perspective does not offer too many disadvantages for learning, perhaps the most important points would be to achieve a good depth and range of movement. It would also be very important to incorporate, although conceptually, the alternation and opposition in the movement of the arms

The student out of the water and standing performs arm circumferences, taking as a reference that the movement is close to the body at all times and that when one arm is raised the other should be driving downwards

In the water and with the help of a partner to hold it by the feet, perform the movement of the arms.

In the water and with a float in the legs, perform the movement of the arms.

Perform a section of the pool with leg movements and working with one arm, the other at the side of the body. Then work with the other.

The same as above but working with one arm, then with the other and then with both (to be able to do a double-stroke back, the swimmer must have a good movement of the legs, otherwise the position of his body in the water will be permanently modified).

Perform the back swim trying to recover the shoulders as much as possible when the arms pass through the perpendicular.

Leg movement:

It would be convenient to allocate some exercises in order for the student to identify and grasp globally how the movement of the legs is, they can be carried out out of the water and accompanied by an explanation and argument by the teacher of the most important points . In this way we would highlight the alternate and opposite character in the movement of the legs and the importance of it coming out of the hip joint in order to ensure the student's understanding of the movement amplitude factor.

Once in contact with the water and sitting on the edge of the pool, perform the movement of the legs

In the water and taking the edge to perform the movement of the legs in the dorsal position, the teacher or a partner may begin to make some corrections regarding range of motion and excessive flexion of the knees.

With the nape of the neck resting on the lifeline, the eyes directed up and forward and with the arms at the side of the body, perform the movement of the legs.

In the dorsal decubitus position and with the arms at the side of the body perform the movement of the legs

Idem anterior but accompany the movement of the legs with the corresponding body roll

Perform leg movement with one arm extended behind and the other at the side of the body

Perform the movement of the legs with both arms extended behind and hands clasped.

Breathing and coordination:

Breathing in the back swim does not offer major inconveniences because the head is always out of the water, however it would be convenient for the student to incorporate a respiratory rhythm. Regarding coordination, it is necessary to permanently emphasize the alternation and opposition in the movement of the arms and not to allow the arms to join the side of the body.

Perhaps the most recommended exercise to include coordination would be to place one arm extended behind and the other at the side of the body and each a predetermined number of leg movements change position of the arms, so on until you swim back without intermediate pauses.

The good result of a learning sequence lies in taking the necessary time to allow the student to understand and elaborate the movement proposal and in my understanding we should give very simple exercises so as not to complicate the student's elaboration. In any case, this form of swimming requires an extremely long time for leg work if the final desire is to have a student who swims well on his back.
