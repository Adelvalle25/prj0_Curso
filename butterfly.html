Butterfly

It is one of the most striking within the discipline of swimming. However, this technique is also one of the most complex, demanding and strenuous, so you have to know its main aspects to execute it correctly.


Techniques:

Armful:

The stroke in the butterfly technique is somewhat similar to that performed in the crawl technique, but instead of doing it alternately, it is performed simultaneously. In the same way, both arms should be slightly flexed when entering water, which is combined with a rotated posture in them, in which the elbows must look up, and the hands out, forming a 45-degree angle. This effect is achieved by the flexion that continually occurs at the elbows. It is important that to complete the stroke, the hands push back, reaching the height of the hips, forming a movement in the form of a circle.

Breathing:

In the butterfly technique, certain head movements must be carried out that will allow the swimmer to breathe properly, recovering his breath to continue his movement in the pool. In this way, when the stroke is making its external sweep, the elevation of the head must be started so that the face is out of the water, and the swimmer manages to take a breath. The swimmer's face must then come out of the water at the same time that the stroke sweeps upward, completing the movement in the first phase of recovery. Later, in the second phase of recovery, the face enters the water again, thus initiating a new swimming cycle. It is also worth noting that breathing should be done every two stroke cycles, although many swimmers do it in each one.

Leg movement:

It is characterized in that its kick is performed at the same time as the stroke. Another aspect to consider in this technique is that, during the kick, both legs move simultaneously, and not alternatively, as would happen in the crawl or back techniques. Finally, it should be borne in mind that the kick in this technique originates from the hips, which is why adequate toning is essential in that segment of the body, to avoid fatigue.
